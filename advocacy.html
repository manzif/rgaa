Advocacy

Licensing

The gaming industry has experienced unprecedented growth and expansion throughout the world. An essential foundation of the success that has led to such enormous growth has been the use of sound legal and regulatory structures. To operate and provide their services in Rwanda, gaming operators need to possess gaming license provided by Ministry of Trade and Industry through Gaming Regulatory Authority.

RGAA plays the role of bridge between gaming operators, regulatory authority and other stakeholders, therefore it advocates its members when it comes to license renewal, disciplinary matters and compliance.

illegal gaming activities

Law No 58/2011 of 31/12/2011 governing gaming activities in Rwanda in article 37 says that without prejudice to provisions of other Laws, a person who directly or indirectly engages in, conducts or makes available a gaming activity that is prohibited by the Law partly or entirely with intention of getting an interest violates this Law and shall be punished.

It has observed that on Rwanda market there are many illegal gaming operators, especially in gaming machine facility category (Slot machine) and this has a significant negative social and economic impact to the country.

Consequently, this negative impact affects legal operators’ businesses and kills the image of gaming activities in general, therefore RGAA puts in its priorities a powerful advocacy, to fight against unlawful gaming activities by working closely with regulatory authority and other stakeholders to apply administrative sanctions as it is cited in article 38 of the abovementioned law.

Regulations

RGAA represents the majority of the approved licensees of the domestic gaming industry in Rwanda. Its objective is to advocate for fair and equitable gaming regulation and legislation, based on factual data and research for the benefits of all its stakeholders.

Gaming tax

The gaming industry is subject to higher levels of taxation than other industries in many countries. RGAA will continue to be a leading advocate for the gaming industry by working with Rwanda Revenue Authority to insure fair gaming taxes.

Modern Gambling

To continue to grow and innovate as a modern entertainment industry, gaming operators must be able to changing consumer demands and address to today’s competitive market realities. Adapting to this fluid market, country vision and leading innovation, RGAA will work closely with regulatory authority to remove barriers to innovation by clarifying and amending internet or online gambling policy.

Applicable gaming business issues

RGAA advocates for a range of issues that affects gaming operator’s business competitiveness including getting performance guarantee, bank loans etc…
